Help — how to use RegMap

RegMap is one platform with two areas: a live security Monitor and an Assess workflow (scan → findings → compliance). Everything runs from this browser.

The tabs

📊 Monitor

Real-time decisioning across the detectors (identity, OT/ICS, compliance). Watch the live event feed, the alert queue, and response actions. Click an alert to open its case: see the evidence, resolve it as true/false positive (which trains the system), suppress a noisy subject, or take a response action.

🛡️ Assess — scan → findings → compliance

A guided, four-part flow:
1. Scan & findings. Choose how to discover assets:
  • Scan from server — point at a host, CIDR, or a public/WAN IP you're authorized to test. (A public IP shows the internet-facing perimeter only — not devices inside a LAN.)
  • Use an agent — to scan a private network (home/office) a cloud console can't reach: click Create agent job, run the one-line command it gives you on a machine inside that network, and results flow back here.
  • Import a report — already ran Nessus, OpenVAS, or nuclei? Upload it; we enrich and rank the findings.
Tick “I am authorized to scan this target”, then scan. You'll get findings: open ports and CVEs, each flagged with KEV (actively exploited) and EPSS (exploit probability) and a blended risk score. You can stop here (“scan only”).
2. Verify. Review the findings — that's the picture the compliance mapping will use.
3. Interview. Click “Generate compliance package” to have a short, natural conversation about your organization (the assistant asks one question at a time).
4. Report. Get prioritized NIST SP 800-53 control recommendations and download DOCX / XLSX / JSON. (Optionally include AI-written policy language.)

Prefer no scan? Pick “Questionnaire only” to generate a compliance template from an expanded questionnaire.

Authorization & safety
Only scan networks you own or are explicitly authorized to test. Response actions that change posture (e.g. disable account) are recorded stubs — nothing is executed against live systems. The AI is advisory only; humans make the decisions.
Tips
• Running in Docker, “Discover environment” shows the container's network; to scan your real LAN, enter your range (e.g. 10.0.0.0/24) or use an agent.
• First interview reply is slower (the language model loads once), then it's quick.
• Large ranges: raise Max hosts, or scan a smaller CIDR.
